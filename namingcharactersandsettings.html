Naming the characters and settings within your story can seem like a daunting task. There is a delicate balance between making sure names fit within the world you've created without them sounding too silly. Here are a few tips on how to name the characters and places in your world!

Your characters will likely be the heart of your story, but coming up with names for them can be a challenge. After all, you probably want the names to fit in with the fantasy setting without them being too incomprehensible. A good way to keep with names of your characters consistent is to look to real world cultures and draw inspriation from them. This is especially helpful if there is a specific country or culture is the basis of your story. Another tip is to take real world names and change the spelling of it slightly. Take the Jon Snow from the A Song of Fire and Ice books and the show Game of Thrones. The Name "John" is a very common name in the English speaking world, but changing the spelling of the name slighlty makes it feel unique to the world that George R.R. Martin created without creating a new name that may be difficult for readers to pronounce.

When writing a fantasy setting, there will likely be a large variety of countries, cities, towns and villages that will need names! A common way to name these places is based on the surronding geography. Is your village near a river? You can call it something like Riverview. Is it located in a dark forest? A name like Shade's Crossing would be perfect! The name of a location should also reflect the size and relative importance within the culture. Trying to name the capital city of a country? The name should be as grand as the city. A small town, however, should be quaint and meek. Names can also be used to convey the overall energy of a setting. A great example of this is the Underdark from the Dungeons and Dragons universe. This name not only incoporates the geography of the location, but also the mood of it: a dark, subterrian environment home to a variety of terrifying aand dangerous creatures. Overall, a location's name should communicate the general "vibe" of the place.

Magic is often one of the most compelling elements in a fantasy story. Writers often have a difficult time crafting magic systems that are unique and interestin while maintaining some sort of logical consisentcy to them. A good way to start building your magic system is to learn the difference between hard magic and soft magic systems. Knowing the difference will allow you to pick one approach, or blend the two in an interesting way.

Hard magic systems are magic systems governed by strict rules and laws. There are specific methods and tools for doing things, and the results are highly predictable with little ambiguity. These rules are typically laid out to the audience through exposition or dialogue she that they may understand these rules just as much as the characters do. One of the most common tropes of hard magic system books is the "magic school", where magical individuals go to learn all of these rules and how to apply them practically. The Harry Potter books/movies are perhaps no of the most famous examples of this; the audience is learning these rules right alongside the characters, makingit an easy method for explaining the magic system within the world.

Unlike hard magic systems, soft magic systems typically do not have defined, logical rules. It is often mysterious and wild, and can be difficult to use or access. While this may seem like lazy writing to some, soft magic systems can be utilized very effectively. If hard magic systems give the audience a sense of logical understanding, soft magic systems can inspire a sense of awe and wonder. Remember, not everyting in your story needs to be explained in detail; some things are simply meant to be expereinced. The Chronicles of Narnia books/movies are a great example of a soft magic system. Not much is explained within the text about how magic works in this world, allowing the audience to experience magic rather than simply study it with the characters.

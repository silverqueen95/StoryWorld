The natural world of your story can be a great worldbuilding tool. Adding unique natural elements can really make your story feel alive. Plants, animals and weather patterns can all of an impact on your story.There are endless possiblites when it comes to writting the natural world!

Biodiversity within your fictional world will help it feel more realistic. Incoporating both real and fictional species adds depth and complexity to your world. There are many established magical creatures you can use, but do not feel like you must limit yourself to using dragons, centaurs and unicorns! Inventing your own fantastical creatures give your story that creative flare that makes it stand out! This principle applies to plant life as well. What kind of fruits, vegtables, and herbs are stables of the citizens'diets? What medicinal uses do the different plants have? What about religious/ceremonial uses? These are important questions to consider when writing.

Weather typically has a significant impact on a region. Snow, floods, droughts, and storms can not only be an obstacle for your characters, but cna also be used to symbolize more internal conflicts within them. It is also important that the climate of a region affects that kind of plants and animals that can live there. For example, you do not typically find cold-blooded reptiles in arctic climates. Like we mentioned in the geographic features section, so regions will have different weather patterns. These conditions will typically affect the types of activites and occupations people are able to participate in within these regions.
